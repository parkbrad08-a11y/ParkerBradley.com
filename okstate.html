Personal Code of Ethics

Truth comes first.

I don't publish what I can't stand behind. If the data doesn't support it, the claim doesn't make it into the caption, the press release or the pitch. Metrics I share are real. The work has to be honest.

Transparency over spin.

If a campaign underperformed, I say so and explain why. Spinning a miss into a win doesn't serve the client, the audience or me. Accountability is how you actually get better.

Audiences aren't targets. They're people.

The people on the other end of my content have real lives and limited attention. I don't manipulate it. I earn it. That means creating something actually worth their time.

AI must serve, not shortcut.

I build with AI every day. That means I'm responsible for what comes out. Not the model. AI pushes my thinking further. It doesn't replace my judgment. I verify. I edit. I own the output.

Protect what's private.

Client data, audience analytics, performance numbers. None of that is mine to spread around. I treat it carefully. What isn't mine to share doesn't get shared.

Represent the profession well.

Communications work shapes how people see the world. That's real influence and not something to throw around. I don't lend it to work I think causes harm, no matter what the opportunity looks like.

Keep learning.

The industry moves fast. What worked last year may not work today. I stay curious, stay critical and don't mistake confidence for expertise.

Be consistent when no one's watching.

This isn't a public statement. It's a standard I hold myself to whether or not anyone's evaluating it.
